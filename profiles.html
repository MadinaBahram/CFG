Ada Lovelace (1815-1852) was an English mathematician and writer. Ada Lovelace has been called the world's first computer programmer, because she wrote the world's first machine algorithm for an early computing machine that existed only on paper. Every second Tuesday in October is known as Ada Lovelace Day to celebrate the achievements of women in STEM careers

Find out more about Ada Lovelace here

Katherine Johnson (1918-2020) was a physicist and mathematician, and one of the first three black students to attend West Virginia college, where she obtained a degree in Mathematics and French. She was known for her work with NASA, manually running equations used for NASA's machines. At age 97, she was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom, America’s highest civilian honour, by President Obama

Read more about Katherine's work here.

Sheryl Sandberg is the Chief Operating Officer of Facebook. Sheryl graduated from Harvard College in 1991 with a B.A in economics, where she also co-founded Women in Economics and Government. She became Facebook's COO in 2007 and led the company from a $56 million loss in 2008 to $18.5 billion in profits in 2019. In 2012, she was named in the Time 100, an annual list of the most influential people in the world.

Read more about Shery Sandberg here.

Grace Hopper (1906-1992) was a computer scientist and one of the first computer programmers to work on the Harvard Mark I. Her work led to the development of COBOL, an early programming language that is still used today. After retiring from the Naval Reserve, she was helped standardise the navy’s computer languages and at the age of 79, she was known as the oldest officer in the navy before she retired in 1986. She was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2016 by Barack Obama.

Find out more about Grace Hopper here.

Susan Wojcicki is CEO of YouTube, which has 2 billion monthly users. She was hired in 1999 as Google employee number 16, and worked on everything from AdSense and Google Analytics to Google Books and Google Images. In 2006 she advocated for the $1.65 billion acquisition of YouTube; the site, which she has run since 2014, is now worth an estimated $90 billion.

You can follow Susan on Twitter for more updates.

Ellen Kangru Pao is an American investor and former CEO of social media company Reddit. She co-founded Project Include – a nonprofit focused on improving diversity in the tech industry. She often speaks publicly on issues like sexism. Pao entered the public spotlight in 2012, when she filed a $16 million lawsuit against her former employer for gender discrimination. Although she lost the case, Pao started a much-needed conversations in the tech world and detailed her experience in her memoir, "Reset: My Fight for Inclusion and Lasting Change."
